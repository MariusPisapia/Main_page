Kiki the young witch

Kiki is a young witch who has just turned thirteen. This is an important date in her family: traditionally at this age, witches have to leave their parents and settle for a year in a new town to perfect their learning.

My neighbor Totoro

Ten-year-old Chihiro Ogino is sulking in the back of her parents' car. Her parents have decided to move to the country, and the little girl misses her old school. While trying to reach their new home, they get lost at the end of a winding road and arrive at a tunnel. Crossing it, despite Chihiro's reluctance, they arrive in what they think is an abandoned theme park.

Spirited away

Tatsuo Kusakabe, a young anthropologist and non-tenured professor at a Tokyo university, arrives in the village where he is going to move with his two daughters, Satsuki, ten, and Mei, four. The little girls joyfully discover their new house, which has not been lived in for some time, and the surrounding countryside where a gigantic camphor tree stands.

The castle in the sky

Pirates of the sky, the "Dora's gang", attack a flying fortress; they are looking for a "flying stone" belonging to a young girl, Sheeta, who is held prisoner. Sheeta manages to escape and ends up with Pazu, a boy her age. They both discover that they have something in common: Laputa, a legendary island floating in the sky.

Why I like this film :

Princess Mononoke is my favorite anime. What I like in this movie is the universe, it's a fantasy universe which takes place in a past period. In this movie the nature is very present, we can find a lot of animals but also spirits from the Japanese mythology. It's an action movie with sequences on a luxuriant forest which seems immense and infinite. The drawings are faithful to the animation of studio Ghibli, the details and the play of light seem real, we have the impression to see a real movie. The main character is a young man who has been cursed and who travels the country following the order of the shaman of his village: "to look at the world without hate". During his journey Ashitaka faces the ravages of war on men but also on nature. The film does not have a moralizing goal but we understand that the author wants to put forward the importance of preserving nature because without it we are nothing.
